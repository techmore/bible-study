What Christians mean by Jesus

Jesus is presented in the New Testament as God’s Son, the Messiah, the crucified and risen Lord, and the one through whom people are forgiven, reconciled to God, and invited into new life.

Daily Listening Path

Stop scrolling. Start listening to Scripture.

The Bible in a Year with Fr. Mike Schmitz is a full 365-episode listening path through the Catholic Bible, with Scripture readings, short commentary, and prayer. It gives people something better to open when they would normally drift into social media.

Open YouTube playlist Listen on Ascension Open reading plan PDF

Why it works

Each daily episode is short enough to replace a scrolling session, but substantial enough to build a real whole-Bible habit over time.

How to use it

  1. Put the playlist or podcast where your social apps usually win.
  2. Listen while walking, driving, cleaning, or winding down.
  3. Keep a Bible nearby when a passage makes you want to slow down.
  4. If you miss days, resume from the next episode instead of quitting.

What to know

This is a Catholic reading plan using the RSV Second Catholic Edition and includes the deuterocanonical books, so it is especially useful for understanding Catholic Scripture practice.

Start Here

If you only do one thing, do this.

1

Read John first

John is the clearest first look at who Jesus is, what he said, and why his life matters.

2

Listen while you read

Hearing the Gospel out loud helps the story land, especially if you are new to Christian language.

3

Compare translations

Use one readable translation and one more literal translation to see nuance.

4

Add context

Learn the Jewish, Roman, Greek, and geographic world behind the text so the stories make sense.

A note on framing

This is meant to be broad, beginner-friendly, and honest.

The goal is not to force a narrow denomination, but to help a new person understand Jesus, the Bible, and Christianity clearly, with enough structure to explore, enough listening options to stay connected, and enough depth to keep learning.
