Progress

How to use the roadmap over years

The flow is the map, but the cadence is what turns it into mastery. Use the lighter nodes for momentum and the denser nodes for deeper seasons of study.

Daily

20 to 45 minutes. Read or listen, write one note, and finish one small action tied to the current unlocked node.

Weekly

2 to 4 hours. Complete one substantial study block, one review session, and one context or theology add-on.

Quarterly

Choose one branch to push deeper, then return to the main path so progress stays connected instead of fragmented.

Mastery model

What the branches are trying to build

The map starts with Jesus, then expands outward into canon, translation, traditions, context, apologetics, and finally teaching and integration.

Core path

John, the Gospels, Acts, canon, translations, and the Bible’s overall storyline.

Addendum unlocks

Christian traditions, historical context, Greek and Latin tools, and apologetics open after the base story is stable.

End state

You can explain Jesus clearly, place books in the story, compare traditions and translations responsibly, and teach others.

Daily assignment

Pick one reading, one listening, one note, and one action.

That keeps the roadmap grounded. Over time, the accumulation of simple reps becomes real mastery.
