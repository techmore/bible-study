Mastery roadmap

A four-year path with daily practice, weekly study, and real specialization.

This page is designed like a skill tree. You can start shallow, deepen over time, and then branch into disciplines like Gospel study, biblical theology, historical context, Greek, apologetics, and teaching.

Enter the tree Pick a specialization

Core idea

The goal is not just reading. It is moving toward mastery: knowing the story, the language, the world, the doctrines, the arguments, and the habits well enough to teach, defend, and live them.

Cadence

Daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly rhythm

A good roadmap should be livable. These rhythms make the path sustainable over years instead of only interesting for a weekend.

Four years

From beginner to capable guide

Each year opens a new layer: first Jesus, then the whole Bible, then the world behind the text, then synthesis and specialization.

Skill tree

Branches you can level up in parallel

Think of this as a set of skill lines. You do not have to finish one before touching another, but each line gets deeper and more demanding as you go.

Specializations

Choose a lane for deeper mastery

These are the equivalent of class specs: focused tracks for people who want to go beyond basic literacy and become especially strong in one area.

Unlocks

Addendum skills that open after the core path

These are the extra branches that should feel earned, not front-loaded. They are the follow-up layer after the basic Gospel path is in place.

Depth

Shallow dives, deep dives, and mastery targets

Use shallow dives when you need momentum. Use deep dives when you want real command of the material.

Daily assignment

Pick one reading, one listening, one note, and one action.

That keeps the roadmap grounded. Over time, the accumulation of simple reps becomes real mastery.
